Fesenjan

Description

Fesenjan is an Iranian traditional khoresh, stew. It is mostly popular in northern part of Iran. In autumns and winters it is made out of wild duck and other ingredients while in springs and summers chicken is used instead of wild duck.

Ingredients

*The amount of ingredients is enough for four to five people.

  • 4 pieces of chest or legs of duck
  • 400 gr grinded walnut
  • 4 tbsp pomegranate paste
  • 1.5 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 onions
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • salt , black pepper, turmeric and oil in the amount needed

Steps

  1. Chop the onions very well and put them with a small amount of oil in a suitable pan. Put the pan on stove and fry the chopped onions.
  2. After couple of minutes when the onions are getting lighter add the spices, duck pieces and fry them very well.
  3. Add three cup of water to the pan let it boil for about half an hour.
  4. Fry on another pan the grinded walnut until its color gets a little darker, now pour a cup of cold water into the pan then let it slowly cooks.
  5. When you see the oil above the boiling walnut it is the time to add pomegranate and tomato paste and let them cook for another 30 minutes.
  6. Now add the cooked duck to the boiling walnut and paste pur two cup of cold water and let them cook for half an hour.
  7. If you want your stew be a little bit sweeter you can add the sugar at the end of the cooking process.
